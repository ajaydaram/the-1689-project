# The Picture of the Gospel: Why the Doctrine of Marriage Is the Believer's Domestic Calling

**The Picture of the Gospel: Why the Doctrine of Marriage Is the Believer's Domestic Calling**

### (1689 Baptist Confession, Chapter 25 – Complete Exposition with Full Footnotes)

### Introduction: The Picture of the Gospel

Chapter 25 of the 1689 Baptist Confession of Faith, entitled "Of Marriage," stands as one of the most culturally urgent and pastorally essential chapters in the entire confession.[^1] If Chapter 21 declares the believer's liberty of conscience, Chapter 22 addresses the worship of God, Chapter 23 the taking of lawful oaths, and Chapter 24 the role of the civil magistrate, then Chapter 25 turns to the **domestic sphere**—the covenant of marriage as God's creation ordinance for the flourishing of human society and as a picture of Christ and His church.[^2]

The chapter is brief but remarkably dense, comprising four paragraphs. Paragraph 1 defines the **essence of marriage**—its exclusive, monogamous, heterosexual character. Paragraph 2 states the **purposes of marriage**—mutual help, procreation, and the prevention of sexual immorality. Paragraph 3 addresses the **permissible parties and prohibited unions**—affirming that believers must marry "in the Lord" and avoid being unequally yoked with unbelievers. Paragraph 4 prohibits **incestuous marriages** within the degrees of consanguinity or affinity forbidden in Scripture.

### Part 1: Exegetical Foundations – The Biblical Shape of Marriage

#### Paragraph 1 – The Essence and Boundaries of Marriage

The confession opens with an emphatic declaration of marriage's fundamental boundaries: <ConfessionBox>
"Marriage is to be between one man and one woman; neither is it lawful for any man to have more than one wife, nor for any woman to have more than one husband at the same time."
</ConfessionBox>

**1. The Exclusive Definition.** "Marriage is to be between one man and one woman."[^8] The confession deliberately rejects every deviation from the creational norm. The institution of marriage is not a human invention subject to redefinition; it is God's creation ordinance, established "from the beginning of the creation" (Mark 10:6). The confession cites Genesis 2:24: "Therefore a man shall leave his father and his mother and hold fast to his wife, and they shall become one flesh."[^9] The Hebrew phrase "ish… ishshah" leaves no room for ambiguity. Jesus Himself grounds marriage in the creational order: "Have you not read that he who created them from the beginning made them male and female" (Matthew 19:4).[^10]

**2. Monogamy, Not Polygamy.** The second clause declares: "Neither is it lawful for any man to have more than one wife, nor for any woman to have more than one husband at the same time."[^11] The confession rejects both polygamy (multiple wives) and polyandry (multiple husbands). While polygamy was practiced by some Old Testament patriarchs, Scripture never endorses it, and its consequences are consistently shown to bring disorder, jealousy, and strife. The marriage relationship is exclusive and lifelong.

**3. The Original Design.** The confession cites Malachi 2:15: "Did he not make them one, with a portion of the Spirit in their union? And what was the one God seeking? Godly offspring."[^12] The prophet explicitly grounds monogamy in God's creative act: God "made them one" and "has the remnant of the Spirit." The Hebrew phrase "the one" may refer to the one man and the one woman. The confession thus affirms that the creational norm is one man and one woman, bound together in a lifelong covenant, reflecting the unity and exclusivity of Christ's relationship with His church (Ephesians 5:31-32).

**4. The Proof of One Flesh.** The confession cites Matthew 19:5-6: "Therefore a man shall leave his father and his mother and hold fast to his wife, and the two shall become one flesh. So they are no longer two but one flesh. What therefore God has joined together, let not man separate."[^13] Jesus elevates marriage above all human redefinition. The "one flesh" union is not a mutable social contract but a divine joining that man may not break.

**Key Scripture Proofs for Paragraph 1:** Genesis 2:24; Malachi 2:15; Matthew 19:5-6.[^14]

#### Paragraph 2 – The Purposes of Marriage

Having established the boundaries of marriage, the confession now declares its purposes: <ConfessionBox>
"Marriage was ordained for the mutual help of husband and wife, for the increase of mankind with a legitimate issue, and the preventing of uncleanness."
</ConfessionBox>

**1. Mutual Help.** "For the mutual help of husband and wife."[^16] The confession cites Genesis 2:18: "Then the LORD God said, 'It is not good that the man should be alone; I will make him a helper fit for him.'"[^17] This is the first purpose stated in Scripture. Adam was created with a need for companionship and assistance that could not be met by any animal. The woman was created as a "helper" (Hebrew *ezer kenegdo*)—one who is both corresponding to him and completing him.

**2. Procreation.** "For the increase of mankind with a legitimate issue."[^18] The confession cites Genesis 1:28: "And God blessed them. And God said to them, 'Be fruitful and multiply and fill the earth and subdue it.'"[^19] The command to procreate was given to humanity as a whole before the fall. Children are a blessing from the Lord (Psalm 127:3-5). The phrase "legitimate issue" guards against any notion that procreation outside the bounds of marriage is permissible.

**3. Prevention of Uncleanness.** "And the preventing of uncleanness."[^20] The confession cites 1 Corinthians 7:2, 9: "But because of the temptation to sexual immorality, each man should have his own wife and each woman her own husband… But if they cannot exercise self-control, they should marry. For it is better to marry than to burn with passion."[^21] Paul's argument is pastoral and practical. Marriage is God's ordained remedy for the powerful sexual impulses that remain in fallen humanity.

**Key Scripture Proofs for Paragraph 2:** Genesis 2:18; Genesis 1:28; 1 Corinthians 7:2,9.[^22]

#### Paragraph 3 – The Lawfulness of Marriage and the Prohibition of Unequal Yoking

Paragraph 3 addresses who may marry and who may not: <ConfessionBox>
"It is lawful for all sorts of people to marry, who are able with judgment to give their consent; yet it is the duty of Christians to marry in the Lord; and therefore such as profess the true religion, should not marry with infidels, or idolaters; neither should such as are godly, be unequally yoked, by marrying with such as are wicked in their life, or maintain damnable heresy."
</ConfessionBox>

**1. The Universal Lawfulness of Marriage.** "It is lawful for all sorts of people to marry, who are able with judgment to give their consent."[^24] The confession cites Hebrews 13:4: "Let marriage be held in honor among all, and let the marriage bed be undefiled, for God will judge the sexually immoral and adulterous."[^25] The qualification "able with judgment to give their consent" forbids forced marriages and establishes the need for rational capacity.

**2. The Duty to Marry "In the Lord."** "Yet it is the duty of Christians to marry in the Lord."[^26] The confession cites 1 Corinthians 7:39: "A wife is bound to her husband as long as he lives. But if her husband dies, she is free to be married to whom she wishes, only in the Lord."[^27] Paul's phrase "only in the Lord" means that a Christian must marry a fellow believer.

**3. The Prohibition of Unequal Yoking.** The confession then draws a sharp line: "Such as profess the true religion, should not marry with infidels, or idolaters; neither should such as are godly, be unequally yoked, by marrying with such as are wicked in their life, or maintain damnable heresy."[^28] The confession cites Nehemiah 13:25-27, where Nehemiah confronts those who had married foreign women and warns that Solomon's foreign wives turned his heart away from God.

**Key Scripture Proofs for Paragraph 3:** Hebrews 13:4; 1 Timothy 4:3; 1 Corinthians 7:39; Nehemiah 13:25-27.[^29]

#### Paragraph 4 – The Prohibition of Incest

The confession concludes with a prohibition of incestuous unions: <ConfessionBox>
"Marriage ought not to be within the degrees of consanguinity or affinity, forbidden in the Word; nor can such incestuous marriages ever be made lawful, by any law of man or consent of parties, so as those persons may live together as man and wife."
</ConfessionBox>

**1. Consanguinity and Affinity.** The confession cites Leviticus 18, the most detailed biblical regulation of prohibited sexual relations. "Consanguinity" refers to blood relations (Latin *con* "with" + *sanguis* "blood"). "Affinity" refers to relationships established by marriage, not blood.[^31]

**2. The Absolute Prohibition.** "Nor can such incestuous marriages ever be made lawful, by any law of man or consent of parties."[^32] The confession rejects the possibility that human legislation could ever legitimize what God has forbidden. The confession cites Mark 6:18 and 1 Corinthians 5:1.[^33]

**Key Scripture Proofs for Paragraph 4:** Leviticus 18; Mark 6:18; 1 Corinthians 5:1.[^34]

### Part 2: Theological Anatomy – The Pillars of a Biblical Doctrine of Marriage

**Pillar 1: Creation Ordained.** Marriage is not a human invention but a divine ordinance established at creation, before the fall, and therefore binding on all humanity.[^35]

**Pillar 2: Heterosexual and Monogamous.** The confession defines marriage exclusively as a union between one man and one woman.[^36]

**Pillar 3: Purpose-Driven.** The three purposes of marriage are mutual help, procreation, and the prevention of sexual immorality.[^37]

**Pillar 4: "In the Lord."** Believers must marry believers. Unequal yoking is prohibited.[^38]

**Pillar 5: Incest Prohibited.** Marriages within the forbidden degrees of consanguinity and affinity are always unlawful.[^39]

### Part 3: Pastoral Diagnosis – The Crises of a Corrupted Marriage

**Crisis 1: The Redefinition of Marriage.** Many in society and even in the church seek to redefine marriage beyond the biblical norm of one man and one woman.[^40]

**Crisis 2: The Neglect of the Purposes of Marriage.** Many marry for purely selfish reasons, ignoring God's design.[^41]

**Crisis 3: The Epidemic of Unequal Yoking.** Many professing Christians marry unbelievers, violating the clear command to marry "in the Lord."[^42]

**Crisis 4: The Blurring of Family Boundaries.** Incestuous relationships, once universally condemned, are being normalized.[^43]

**Crisis 5: The Loss of Marriage as a Picture of the Gospel.** Marriage is meant to display the relationship between Christ and His church (Ephesians 5:31-32).[^44]

### Part 4: Practical Return – Living in Light of Marriage

**Step 1: Honor Marriage.** Recognize that marriage is God's creation ordinance, not a human institution subject to redefinition.[^45]

**Step 2: Embrace the Purposes of Marriage.** Seek mutual help, welcome children, and use marriage as God's remedy for sexual temptation.[^46]

**Step 3: Marry Only in the Lord.** If you are unmarried, do not be unequally yoked. Marry a fellow believer.[^47]

**Step 4: Reject Incestuous Unions.** Do not excuse relationships that Scripture forbids.[^48]

**Step 5: See Marriage as a Picture of the Gospel.** For those who are married, let your marriage display the love of Christ for His church.[^49]

### Conclusion: The Picture of the Gospel

Chapter 25 is the believer's domestic calling. Marriage is God's creation ordinance—one man and one woman in a lifelong covenant. Its purposes are mutual help, the increase of mankind, and the prevention of sexual immorality. Believers must marry "in the Lord," avoiding unequal yoking. And incestuous unions remain forever forbidden. The same God who established marriage also defines its boundaries, and the same Christ who loves His church is the pattern for every Christian marriage.[^50]

**A Prayer from Chapter 25:**

*O God, who from the beginning created them male and female: We thank Thee for the gift of marriage, ordained for our mutual help, for the increase of mankind, and for the prevention of uncleanness. We confess that we have often sought to redefine Thy design, to neglect Thy purposes, and to enter into unequal yokes. Forgive us. Grant us to honor marriage as Thy creation ordinance, to marry only in the Lord, and to reject all unions that Thy Word forbids. And let our marriages display the love of Christ for His church. For Christ's sake. Amen.*[^51]
