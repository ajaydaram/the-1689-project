# The Gathered Life: Why the Communion of Saints Is the Believer's Foretaste of Glory

### (1689 Baptist Confession, Chapter 27 – Complete Exposition with Full Footnotes)

Chapter 27 of the 1689 Baptist Confession of Faith, entitled "Of the Communion of Saints," stands as a brief but profoundly rich declaration of the believer's relationship not only to Christ but also to every other believer.[^1] If Chapter 26 describes the church as the gathered assembly of visible saints, Chapter 27 now explores the *life of that assembly* — the mutual fellowship, sharing, and obligation that flows from union with Christ.[^2]

The chapter comprises only two paragraphs, making it one of the shortest in the confession.[^3] Paragraph 1 establishes the **vertical foundation** of communion: union with Christ, which grounds every believer's sharing in His graces, sufferings, death, resurrection, and glory. Paragraph 2 then draws the **horizontal implication**: all saints are bound to maintain fellowship in worship and mutual edification, to serve one another according to the gifts they have received, and to walk together in love, while recognizing the necessary limitations of such communion.[^4]

As one exposition notes, "In the Communion of the Saints, we see not only a union to Christ, in all the benefits of His person and work as mediator, but we see a communion to His Saints as well, as we share in one another's gifts and graces; and all with the common end which is to edify the body of Christ."[^5] The chapter thus answers three crucial questions: What do believers share? What must they do for one another? And what are the limits of this communion?[^6]

### Part 1: Exegetical Foundations – The Biblical Shape of the Communion of Saints

Chapter 27 is a masterful distillation of Scripture's teaching on the mutual fellowship of believers.[^7]

#### Paragraph 1 – The Foundation of Communion: Union with Christ

The confession opens: <ConfessionBox>
"All saints that are united to Jesus Christ, their head, by his Spirit, and faith, although they are not made thereby one person with him, have fellowship in his graces, sufferings, death, resurrection, and glory; and being united to one another in love, they have communion in each other's gifts and graces, and are obliged to the performance of such duties, public and private, as do conduce to their mutual good, both in the inward and outward man."
</ConfessionBox>[^8]

**1. "All saints that are united to Jesus Christ."** The confession grounds communion in union with Christ. All true believers — all the elect — are united to Christ as the head of the church. This is the objective reality that makes communion possible.

**2. "By his Spirit, and faith."** The confession identifies the two means of this union: the Holy Spirit (who effectually applies Christ to the believer) and faith (the instrument by which the believer receives Christ). Sam Waldron, the great expositor, emphasized that "though the elect are one with Christ in the plan of God from all eternity, vital and saving union with Christ is only realized in their experience through the indwelling of the Spirit and the exercise of faith."[^9]

**3. "Although they are not made thereby one person with him."** This clause guards against a critical error. The confession explicitly rejects any notion of a mystical absorption into the divine essence — the heresy of pantheism or deification. The believer does not become God, nor does the believer merge into Christ's person. The union is spiritual and vital, not essential or personal.[^10]

**4. "Have fellowship in his graces, sufferings, death, resurrection, and glory."** The confession lists the five-fold sharing that flows from union with Christ. This is the "communion" itself.

- **Graces** — Believers share in the grace of Christ: wisdom, righteousness, sanctification, and redemption (1 Corinthians 1:30). The confession cites 1 John 1:3, 6-7, and Ephesians 3:8.[^11]

- **Sufferings** — Believers are called to share in Christ's sufferings. The confession cites Philippians 3:10, Romans 6:3-5, and 1 Peter 4:13. Paul counted everything as loss "that I may know him and the power of his resurrection, and may share his sufferings, becoming like him in his death."[^12]

- **Death** — Believers die with Christ. Romans 6:3-5 declares, "Do you not know that all of us who have been baptized into Christ Jesus were baptized into his death?"[^13]

- **Resurrection** — Believers will share in Christ's resurrection. The confession cites Romans 6:5: "For if we have been united with him in a death like his, we shall certainly be united with him in a resurrection like his."[^14]

- **Glory** — Believers will share in Christ's glory. Romans 8:17 declares, "and if children, then heirs — heirs of God and fellow heirs with Christ, provided we suffer with him in order that we may also be glorified with him."[^15]

**5. "And being united to one another in love."** Because believers are united to Christ, they are also united to one another. As the confession states, union with Christ produces union with all other saints. The confession cites 1 John 3:17 and Ephesians 4:15-16.[^16]

**6. "They have communion in each other's gifts and graces."** Believers share not only in Christ's blessings but also in the gifts and graces of their fellow believers. This is the mutual indwelling of the body of Christ.

**7. "And are obliged to the performance of such duties, public and private, as do conduce to their mutual good, both in the inward and outward man."** The confession grounds the mutual obligations of believers in the reality of communion. The confession cites 1 Thessalonians 5:11, 14; Romans 1:12; 1 John 3:16-18; and Galatians 6:10.[^17]

#### Paragraph 2 – The Expression and Limits of Communion

Paragraph 2 states: <ConfessionBox>
"Saints by profession are bound to maintain a holy fellowship and communion in the worship of God, and in performing such other spiritual services as tend to their mutual edification; as also in relieving each other in outward things, according to their several abilities and necessities. Which communion, as God offereth opportunity, is to be extended unto all those who in every place call upon the name of the Lord Jesus."
</ConfessionBox>[^19]

**1. "Saints by profession are bound to maintain a holy fellowship and communion in the worship of God."** The confession grounds the visible expression of communion in the worship of the gathered church. Acts 2:42 describes the early church as "devoting themselves to the apostles' teaching and the fellowship, to the breaking of bread and the prayers."[^20]

**2. "And in performing such other spiritual services as tend to their mutual edification."** The confession extends communion beyond corporate worship to all spiritual services that build up the body. Romans 1:12 speaks of being "mutually encouraged by each other's faith."[^21]

**3. "As also in relieving each other in outward things, according to their several abilities and necessities."** Communion is not merely spiritual; it includes material sharing. The confession cites Romans 12:4-8 and Acts 2:44-45; 4:34-35, where believers sold their possessions and distributed to anyone in need.[^22]

**4. "Which communion... is to be extended unto all those who in every place call upon the name of the Lord Jesus."** The confession declares that communion is not limited to one's local congregation. It extends to all believers everywhere. Romans 1:12; Acts 2:42; 1 John 1:3; and 2 Corinthians 8-9 are cited as proof.[^23]

**5. The Necessary Limitations.** While the confession does not enumerate limitations, Reformed expositors have identified the boundaries of this communion. In his *Modern Exposition*, Samuel Waldron, the great expositor, emphasized that the communion of saints does not imply "a communistic society" in the political sense. There is no divinely sanctioned economic redistribution imposed by force. Paul writes in 2 Thessalonians 3:10, "If anyone is not willing to work, let him not eat." The church is called to care for its poor, but this care respects individual responsibility and does not abolish private property.[^24]

### Part 2: Theological Anatomy – The Pillars of the Communion of Saints

Several theological pillars support the confession's doctrine of the communion of saints.[^26]

**Pillar 1: Union with Christ as the Foundation.** Communion among believers is not a human social construct; it is the necessary consequence of union with Christ. Because believers are united to Christ, they are united to everyone else united to Him.[^27]

**Pillar 2: Spiritual, Not Essential, Union.** The confession explicitly rejects any notion that believers become one person with Christ or that they are absorbed into the divine essence. The distinction between Creator and creature is maintained.[^28]

**Pillar 3: The Five-Fold Sharing.** The confession identifies five specific things believers share in Christ: His graces, sufferings, death, resurrection, and glory. This comprehensive list covers the whole range of redemptive blessing.[^29]

**Pillar 4: Mutual Obligations.** Communion entails duties. Believers are "obliged to the performance of such duties" as conduce to mutual good. The confession does not permit an isolated, individualistic Christianity.

**Pillar 5: Spiritual and Material Sharing.** Communion includes both spiritual services (worship, prayer, teaching, admonition) and material relief (sharing resources with those in need).

**Pillar 6: Universal Extent.** Communion is not limited to the local congregation. It extends to all who call on the name of the Lord Jesus everywhere.

**Pillar 7: Necessary Limitations.** While the confession does not detail limitations, the Reformed tradition has consistently affirmed that this communion does not abolish individual responsibility, private property, or the distinction between voluntary charity and coercive redistribution.[^30]

### Part 3: Pastoral Diagnosis – The Crises of a Neglected Communion

Several contemporary crises are directly addressed by Chapter 27.

**Crisis 1: Individualistic Christianity.** Many believers view their faith as a private matter between themselves and God. The confession declares that communion is not optional; it is a binding obligation.

**Crisis 2: The Neglect of Corporate Worship.** In an age of online streaming and consumer Christianity, many professing believers neglect the gathering of the saints. Paragraph 2 calls believers to maintain "a holy fellowship and communion in the worship of God."

**Crisis 3: The Failure to Share Material Resources.** Many churches are wealthy yet their members are indifferent to the needs of others. The confession grounds material sharing in the reality of spiritual communion.

**Crisis 4: The Myth of Self-Sufficiency.** Believers often refuse to receive help from others, imagining that to ask for help is to admit weakness. The confession teaches that communion means receiving from others as well as giving.

**Crisis 5: The Local Church as the Only Church.** Some Christians have no sense of connection to believers outside their own congregation. The confession declares that communion is to be extended "to all those who in every place call upon the name of the Lord Jesus."

### Part 4: Practical Return – Living in Light of the Communion of Saints

**Step 1: Rejoice in Your Union with Christ.** Your salvation is not a private transaction between you and God. You have been united to Christ and therefore to every other believer.

**Step 2: Share in the Sufferings of Your Brothers and Sisters.** When a fellow believer suffers, you are called to suffer with them. Reach out to those who are hurting.

**Step 3: Serve the Church with Your Gifts.** Every believer has been given gifts for the common good. Do not hoard your gifts; use them to edify the body.

**Step 4: Give Generously to Those in Need.** The confession calls for relieving "each other in outward things." Give according to your ability to those who are in need.

**Step 5: Gather with the Saints.** Do not neglect the public worship of God. The confession declares that believers are "bound to maintain a holy fellowship and communion in the worship of God."

**Step 6: Extend Communion Beyond Your Local Church.** Pray for believers around the world. Support missionaries. Give to churches in other places. The communion of saints knows no geographical boundaries.

**Step 7: Distinguish Between Communion and Communalism.** The communion of saints is not a justification for political communism or the abolition of private property. It is a voluntary, Spirit-wrought sharing that respects individual responsibility and the principle of working for one's living.[^31]

### Conclusion: The Gathered Life

Chapter 27 of the 1689 Baptist Confession is not a museum piece of seventeenth-century theology. It is the believer's foretaste of glory. The communion of saints is not a human social arrangement but a divine reality flowing from union with Christ. Believers share in His graces, sufferings, death, resurrection, and glory. They are united to one another in love. They are bound to maintain fellowship in worship, to serve one another with their gifts, to share their resources, and to walk together in love. And this communion extends to all who call on the name of the Lord Jesus, everywhere.[^32]

**A Prayer from Chapter 27** (adapted from the Puritan tradition):

*O Christ, the Head of the church, by whose Spirit we are united to Thee: We thank Thee for the communion of saints. We thank Thee that Thou dost not save us in isolation but as members of one body. We thank Thee for the privilege of sharing in Thy graces, sufferings, death, resurrection, and glory. We thank Thee for the gift of fellow believers, who bear our burdens, share their gifts, and walk with us in love. Forgive us for the times we have neglected the gathering of the saints, hoarded our gifts, and closed our hearts to those in need. Grant us to maintain a holy fellowship in worship, to serve one another with cheerfulness, and to extend communion to all who call upon Thy name. Until we share in Thy glory, let us share in the gathered life of Thy church. For Thy name's sake. Amen.*[^33]
