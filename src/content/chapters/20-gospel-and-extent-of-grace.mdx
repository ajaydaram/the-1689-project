# The Voice That Will Not Return Empty: Why the Gospel Must Be Preached to Every Creature

Chapter 20 of the 1689 Baptist Confession of Faith, entitled "Of the Gospel and of the Extent of the Grace Thereof," serves as a bridge between the objective law of God (Chapter 19) and the subjective experience of Christian liberty (Chapter 21).[^1] Having explained the continuing obligation of the moral law, the confession now turns to the *proclamation of the gospel*—the good news that offers salvation freely to all who hear.[^2] The chapter also addresses the relationship between the Old and New Testament dispensations, affirming that the substance of the gospel has always been the same, even though its administration has changed.

The chapter is divided into four paragraphs, which can be grouped under three headings: (1) the nature of the gospel as the sole means of salvation after the fall (paragraph 1); (2) the free offer of the gospel to all nations and the responsibility of those who hear it (paragraph 2); and (3) the difference between the Old and New Testament dispensations of the gospel (paragraphs 3-4).[^3]

This chapter stands against two errors. The first is **hyper-Calvinism**, which denies that the gospel should be preached indiscriminately to all.[^4] The second is **Arminianism**, which denies the effectual, distinguishing grace that alone brings sinners to faith.[^5] The confession carefully affirms both a universal offer of the gospel and a particular effectual call.

### Part 1: Exegetical Foundations – The Biblical Shape of the Gospel and Its Extent

Chapter 20 is a masterful digest of Scripture's teaching on the nature, offer, and history of the gospel.

#### Paragraph 1 – The Sole Means of Salvation after the Fall

The confession opens by declaring the necessity of the gospel: **"The covenant of works being broken by sin, and made unprofitable unto life, God was pleased to give forth the promise of Christ, the seed of the woman, as the means of calling the elect, and begetting in them faith and repentance; in this promise the gospel, as to the substance of it, was revealed, and therein effectual for the conversion and salvation of sinners."**[^6]

**1. The Failure of the Covenant of Works.** "The covenant of works being broken by sin, and made unprofitable unto life."[^7] After the fall, no one could be saved by the covenant of works. Adam's disobedience had rendered that covenant "unprofitable unto life."[^8] Romans 8:3 declares, "For God has done what the law, weakened by the flesh, could not do. By sending his own Son in the likeness of sinful flesh and for sin, he condemned sin in the flesh."[^9] The confession thus establishes the necessity of a new covenant.

**2. The First Gospel Promise.** "God was pleased to give forth the promise of Christ, the seed of the woman."[^10] This refers to the *protoevangelium* of Genesis 3:15: "I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your offspring and her offspring; he shall bruise your head, and you shall bruise his heel."[^11] As Sam Waldron notes, "As early as Genesis 3:15, the gospel was revealed in substance. It was effectual for the salvation of sinners, as the history of Abel, Enoch, Noah, Abraham, and all the Old Testament saints demonstrates."[^12]

**3. The Gospel as the Means of Calling the Elect.** "As the means of calling the elect, and begetting in them faith and repentance."[^13] The gospel is the instrument through which the Spirit effectually calls the elect. Romans 1:16 declares, "The gospel is the power of God for salvation to everyone who believes, to the Jew first and also to the Greek."[^14]

**4. The Substantial Identity of the Gospel in All Ages.** "In this promise the gospel, as to the substance of it, was revealed, and therein effectual for the conversion and salvation of sinners."[^15] The confession affirms that the gospel has been substantially the same from the beginning. As one expositor explains, "The Confession sets forth that the substance of the gospel is the promise of Christ, the seed of the woman, who would crush the serpent's head. This promise was the means of calling the elect and begetting in them faith and repentance."[^16]

**Key Scripture Proofs for Paragraph 1:** Genesis 3:15; Romans 1:16; 1 Corinthians 1:21.[^17]

#### Paragraph 2 – The Free Offer of the Gospel to All Nations

Paragraph 2 addresses the universal proclamation of the gospel: **"This promise of Christ, and salvation by him, is revealed only by the Word of God; neither are the works of creation or providence, with the light of nature, sufficient to make known Christ and his grace, so as to be effectual unto salvation; much less are they, that leave men inexcusable, yet the Lord is pleased to make known his gospel in sundry times and in divers manners, giving forth the promises, and the Spirit by the word, to work faith and conversion in all whom he is pleased to effectually call. This gospel is the only outward means of revealing Christ and his grace, and it is made the power of God unto salvation to all that believe; and it is, in every age, to be preached to all men everywhere, as the duty and privilege of all to whom God shall send them; and no addition nor alteration is to be made therein."**[^18]

**1. The Insufficiency of Natural Revelation.** "Neither are the works of creation or providence, with the light of nature, sufficient to make known Christ and his grace, so as to be effectual unto salvation."[^19] The confession cites Romans 1:19-21, which declares that natural revelation makes God's eternal power and divine nature known, leaving men without excuse, but it does not reveal Christ.[^20] Romans 10:14-15 asks, "How are they to believe in him of whom they have never heard? And how are they to hear without someone preaching?"[^21]

**2. The Sufficiency of the Gospel as the Outward Means.** "The gospel is the only outward means of revealing Christ and his grace, and it is made the power of God unto salvation to all that believe."[^22] Romans 1:16 declares, "The gospel is the power of God for salvation to everyone who believes."[^23] The confession affirms that the gospel is not merely information; it is *power*—the instrument through which God effectually saves.

**3. The Universal Preaching of the Gospel.** "It is, in every age, to be preached to all men everywhere, as the duty and privilege of all to whom God shall send them."[^24] This is the free offer of the gospel. The confession rejects hyper-Calvinism, which would limit the preaching of the gospel to the elect only.[^25] Matthew 28:19-20 commands, "Go therefore and make disciples of all nations." Mark 16:15 declares, "Go into all the world and proclaim the gospel to the whole creation."[^26]

**4. No Addition or Alteration.** "No addition nor alteration is to be made therein."[^27] Galatians 1:8-9 pronounces a curse on anyone who preaches a different gospel.[^28]

**Key Scripture Proofs for Paragraph 2:** Romans 1:19-21; Romans 10:14-15; Romans 1:16; Matthew 28:19-20; Mark 16:15; Galatians 1:8-9.[^29]

#### Paragraph 3 – The Old and New Dispensations: Greater Fulness

Paragraph 3 begins the comparison between the Old and New Testament administrations of the gospel: **"Although this gospel be a mystery, yet it has been in all ages revealed and preached, in sundry ways, progressively, first more obscurely, and after the incarnation of Christ, with more clearness and evidence; yet the grace thereby exhibited is in and by it, and that unto all nations, held forth in the Old Testament, by promises, types, and sacrifices, but in the New Testament by the substance itself, Christ; and this with much more fulness, evidence, spiritual efficacy, and facility, than in the times of old."**[^30]

**1. Progressive Revelation.** "Revealed and preached, in sundry ways, progressively, first more obscurely, and after the incarnation of Christ, with more clearness and evidence."[^31] The confession affirms the progressive nature of revelation. Hebrews 1:1-2 declares, "Long ago, at many times and in many ways, God spoke to our fathers by the prophets, but in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son."[^32] The gospel was not hidden in the Old Testament; it was revealed, but more obscurely, through promises, types, and sacrifices.[^33]

**2. The Substance vs. Shadows.** "Held forth in the Old Testament, by promises, types, and sacrifices, but in the New Testament by the substance itself, Christ."[^34] Colossians 2:17 declares that the Old Testament ceremonies were "a shadow of the things to come, but the substance belongs to Christ."[^35] The confession thus maintains the unity of the covenant of grace while acknowledging the difference between promise and fulfillment.

**3. Greater Fulness, Evidence, Spiritual Efficacy, and Facility.** The New Testament administration is superior in four ways: (a) *fulness*—more complete revelation; (b) *evidence*—clearer manifestation; (c) *spiritual efficacy*—greater power of the Spirit; (d) *facility*—ease of access.[^36] 2 Corinthians 3:18 speaks of beholding the glory of the Lord with unveiled face.[^37]

**Key Scripture Proofs for Paragraph 3:** Hebrews 1:1-2; Colossians 2:17; 2 Corinthians 3:18.[^38]

#### Paragraph 4 – The Abiding Ordinances of the New Testament

The confession concludes with a description of the New Testament administration: **"The dispensation of this gospel, as to the administration of it under the New Testament, is committed only to the word of Christ, the ministry whereof is a standing office in the church of God, and is confined to the preaching of the word, prayer, and administration of the two ordinances of baptism and the Lord's supper, which are the ordinary means of grace and salvation, to be continued to the end of the world, together with the observation of the Lord's day."**[^39]

**1. The Commitment of the Gospel to the Word of Christ.** "The dispensation of this gospel, as to the administration of it under the New Testament, is committed only to the word of Christ."[^40] This does not mean that Scripture alone is the means, but that the gospel is committed to the Word as the authoritative standard.[^41]

**2. The Standing Office of the Ministry.** "The ministry whereof is a standing office in the church of God."[^42] The confession affirms the perpetual office of gospel ministry. Ephesians 4:11-12 declares that Christ gave "the apostles, the prophets, the evangelists, the shepherds and teachers, to equip the saints for the work of ministry, for building up the body of Christ."[^43]

**3. The Ordinary Means of Grace.** The confession lists four elements that constitute the ordinary means of grace: (a) the preaching of the Word; (b) prayer; (c) baptism; (d) the Lord's supper.[^44] These are "the ordinary means of grace and salvation, to be continued to the end of the world."[^45] Acts 2:42 describes the early church as "devoting themselves to the apostles' teaching and the fellowship, to the breaking of bread and the prayers."[^46]

**4. The Observance of the Lord's Day.** The confession adds "together with the observation of the Lord's day."[^47] This refers to the Christian Sabbath, further elaborated in Chapter 22. Revelation 1:10 speaks of "the Lord's day."[^48] 1 Corinthians 16:2 mentions the first day of the week as the day for gathering.[^49]

**Key Scripture Proofs for Paragraph 4:** Ephesians 4:11-12; Acts 2:42; Revelation 1:10; 1 Corinthians 16:2.[^50]

### Part 2: Theological Anatomy – The Pillars of the Gospel and Its Extent

Several theological pillars support the confession's doctrine of the gospel and its extent.

**Pillar 1: The Gospel Is the Sole Means of Salvation after the Fall.** The covenant of works is broken; only the gospel can save. The promise of Genesis 3:15 contains the substance of the gospel.[^51]

**Pillar 2: Natural Revelation Is Insufficient for Salvation.** The works of creation and providence reveal God's existence and moral character, leaving men inexcusable, but they cannot reveal Christ or the way of salvation.[^52]

**Pillar 3: The Gospel Must Be Preached to All.** The confession affirms the free offer of the gospel to all nations, rejecting hyper-Calvinism. The same chapter that affirms particular election (Chapter 3) also affirms universal preaching.[^53]

**Pillar 4: The Gospel Is the Power of God unto Salvation.** The gospel is not merely information; it is the instrument through which the Spirit effectually calls the elect.[^54]

**Pillar 5: Progressive Revelation, One Gospel.** The gospel was revealed in the Old Testament, though more obscurely, through promises, types, and sacrifices. The New Testament reveals the substance, Christ, with greater fulness, evidence, spiritual efficacy, and facility.[^55]

**Pillar 6: The Ordinances of the New Testament Are Abiding.** The ministry of the Word, prayer, baptism, the Lord's supper, and the observance of the Lord's day are the ordinary means of grace, to be continued until the end of the world.[^56]

### Part 3: Pastoral Diagnosis – The Crises of a Distorted View of the Gospel

Several contemporary crises are directly addressed by Chapter 20.

**Crisis 1: Hyper-Calvinism.** Some Reformed believers argue that the gospel should only be preached to the elect. The confession explicitly commands that the gospel "is, in every age, to be preached to all men everywhere."[^57]

**Crisis 2: The Denial of the Free Offer.** Related to hyper-Calvinism, some deny that God sincerely offers salvation to all who hear the gospel. The confession's language of "preached to all men everywhere" affirms the free offer.[^58]

**Crisis 3: The Neglect of Gospel Preaching.** Some churches replace gospel preaching with social action, therapy, or entertainment. The confession declares that the ministry of the Word is a "standing office" and that preaching is an "ordinary means of grace."[^59]

**Crisis 4: The Discontinuity Between Old and New Testaments.** Some dispensationalists so separate the Testaments that they deny that Old Testament saints were saved by the gospel. The confession affirms that the gospel "has been in all ages revealed and preached" and that Old Testament believers were saved by the same grace.[^60]

**Crisis 5: The Neglect of the Ordinances.** Many churches neglect baptism and the Lord's supper, treating them as optional. The confession identifies them as "ordinary means of grace and salvation, to be continued to the end of the world."[^61]

### Part 4: Practical Return – Living in Light of the Gospel

**Step 1: Preach the Gospel to All.** Do not limit the gospel offer to those who seem elect. You do not know who the elect are. Preach freely to all, for the gospel is the power of God unto salvation.[^62]

**Step 2: Trust the Gospel, Not Natural Revelation.** Do not assume that unregenerate people can come to Christ through general revelation alone. They need the specific preaching of the gospel.[^63]

**Step 3: Treasure the Greater Fulness of the New Testament.** Rejoice that you live after the incarnation. You have the substance, not merely the shadows. The veil is torn. The Spirit is poured out.[^64]

**Step 4: Use the Ordinary Means of Grace.** Commit yourself to the preaching of the Word, prayer, baptism, the Lord's supper, and the Lord's day. These are not optional extras; they are the ordinary means through which God saves and sanctifies.[^65]

**Step 5: Guard the Purity of the Gospel.** Do not add to or alter the gospel. Any addition—works, human traditions, or speculative doctrines—is a different gospel.[^66]

**Step 6: Rejoice in the Universal Offer.** Take comfort that the gospel is preached to all. If you are unsure of your election, do not look for your name in the book of life. Look to the promise: "Whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life" (John 3:16).[^67]

**Step 7: Honor the Ministry of the Word.** Support and pray for those who preach the gospel. The ministry is a "standing office" appointed by Christ for the building up of His church.[^68]

### Conclusion: The Voice That Will Not Return Empty

Chapter 20 of the 1689 Baptist Confession is not a museum piece of seventeenth-century theology. It is the church's commissioning to proclaim the gospel to every creature. The gospel is the sole means of salvation after the fall. It is the power of God unto salvation to all who believe. It must be preached to all nations, without addition or alteration. Though it was revealed more obscurely in the Old Testament, it is now revealed with full clarity and spiritual efficacy in the New. And the ordinary means of grace—preaching, prayer, baptism, the Lord's supper, and the Lord's day—are to be continued to the end of the world.[^69]

This is the voice that will not return empty. As Isaiah 55:11 declares, "So shall my word be that goes out from my mouth; it shall not return to me empty, but it shall accomplish that which I purpose, and shall succeed in the thing for which I sent it."[^70]

**A Prayer from Chapter 20** (adapted from the Puritan tradition):

*O God, who in sundry times and in divers manners spoke to our fathers by the prophets, and in these last days has spoken to us by Thy Son: We thank Thee for the gospel, the power of God unto salvation to everyone who believes. We thank Thee that the promise of Christ, the seed of the woman, was given from the beginning, and that it has been effectual for the conversion and salvation of sinners in every age. We thank Thee for the greater fulness, evidence, spiritual efficacy, and facility of the New Testament dispensation.*

*Grant us to preach the gospel to all men everywhere, to all nations, to the whole creation. Keep us from adding to or altering this gospel. And use the ordinary means of grace—the preaching of the Word, prayer, baptism, the Lord's supper, and the Lord's day—to call Thy elect, to nourish their faith, and to build up Thy church until the end of the world. For Christ's sake. Amen.*[^71]
