## The Day That Rights Every Wrong: Why the Last Judgment Is the Believer's Hope and the Unbeliever's Fear

### (1689 Baptist Confession, Chapter 32 – Complete Exposition with Full Footnotes)


### Introduction: The Culmination of All Things

Chapter 32 of the 1689 Baptist Confession of Faith, entitled “Of the Last Judgment,” is the final chapter of the confession.¹ As the capstone of the entire document, it brings the confession to its proper conclusion: the day when every wrong will be righted, every secret revealed, and God’s justice and mercy displayed before the assembled universe.²

The chapter comprises three paragraphs. Paragraph 1 declares the **certainty and nature** of the last judgment: it is appointed by God, executed by Christ, and universal in scope—including apostate angels, all persons who have ever lived, every thought, word, and deed. Paragraph 2 declares the **purpose** of the last judgment: the manifestation of God’s mercy in the eternal salvation of the elect and of His justice in the eternal damnation of the reprobate. Paragraph 3 declares the **present impact** of the last judgment: it deters all men from sin, consoles the godly in their adversity, and calls believers to watchfulness, because the day is certain yet its hour is unknown.³

The chapter concludes the confession with a prayerful doxology: “Come, Lord Jesus; come quickly” (Revelation 22:20).⁴


### Part 1: Exegetical Foundations – The Biblical Shape of the Last Judgment

Chapter 32 is a masterful digest of Scripture’s teaching on the final judgment, drawing especially on Acts 17:31, John 5:22-27, 2 Corinthians 5:10, Romans 2:5-16, Matthew 25:31-46, 2 Thessalonians 1:5-10, and Revelation 20:11-15.⁵


#### Paragraph 1 – The Appointment, Author, Judge, Subjects, and Process of the Last Judgment

The confession opens with a declaration of the certainty and nature of the final judgment: **“God hath appointed a day wherein he will judge the world in righteousness, by Jesus Christ; to whom all power and judgment is given of the Father; in which day, not only the apostate angels shall be judged, but likewise all persons that have lived upon the earth shall appear before the tribunal of Christ, to give an account of their thoughts, words, and deeds, and to receive according to what they have done in the body, whether good or evil.”**⁶

**1. “God hath appointed a day.”** The confession begins with the certainty of the judgment. Acts 17:31 declares, “He has fixed a day on which he will judge the world in righteousness by a man whom he has appointed; and of this he has given assurance to all by raising him from the dead.”⁷ God Himself has set the day—not in the calendar of men, but in the decree of eternity.⁸ As one commentary notes, “It is certain that this day will come because God has determined and appointed it.”⁹

**2. “Judge the world in righteousness.”** The judgment is not arbitrary or capricious; it is “in righteousness.” Romans 2:5 speaks of “the day of wrath when God’s righteous judgment will be revealed.”¹⁰

**3. “By Jesus Christ; to whom all power and judgment is given of the Father.”** Christ is the appointed Judge. John 5:22, 27 records Jesus’ words: “The Father judges no one, but has given all judgment to the Son… and he has given him authority to execute judgment, because he is the Son of Man.”¹¹

**4. “Not only the apostate angels shall be judged.”** The judgment extends to fallen angels. 1 Corinthians 6:3 declares, “Do you not know that we are to judge angels?”¹² Jude 6 speaks of “the angels who did not stay within their own position of authority, but left their proper dwelling, he has kept in eternal chains under gloomy darkness until the judgment of the great day.”¹³

**5. “But likewise all persons that have lived upon the earth shall appear before the tribunal of Christ.”** The confession affirms the universality of the judgment. 2 Corinthians 5:10 declares, “For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ, so that each one may receive what is due for what he has done in the body, whether good or evil.”¹⁴

**6. “To give an account of their thoughts, words, and deeds.”** The judgment extends to the inward life as well as the outward. Matthew 12:36 records Jesus’ words: “I tell you, on the day of judgment people will give account for every careless word they speak.”¹⁵ Ecclesiastes 12:14 declares, “For God will bring every deed into judgment, with every secret thing, whether good or evil.”¹⁶

**7. “To receive according to what they have done in the body, whether good or evil.”** The confession affirms that judgment will be according to works—not that works are the ground of justification, but that they are the evidence of the state of the soul. As one commentary notes, “The Day of Judgment is not the day that will determine the destinies of men; their destinies were fixed at the time they died (Hebrews 9:27).”¹⁷

**Key Scripture Proofs for Paragraph 1:** Acts 17:31; John 5:22, 27; 1 Corinthians 6:3; Jude 6; 2 Corinthians 5:10; Ecclesiastes 12:14; Matthew 12:36; Romans 14:10, 12; Matthew 25:32-46.¹⁸


#### Paragraph 2 – The Purpose of the Last Judgment: The Manifestation of God’s Glory in Mercy and Justice

Paragraph 2 declares the ultimate purpose of the judgment: **“The end of God’s appointing this day, is for the manifestation of the glory of his mercy, in the eternal salvation of the elect; and of his justice, in the eternal damnation of the reprobate, who are wicked and disobedient; for then shall the righteous go into everlasting life, and receive that fulness of joy and glory with everlasting rewards, in the presence of the Lord; but the wicked, who know not God, and obey not the gospel of Jesus Christ, shall be cast aside into everlasting torments, and punished with everlasting destruction, from the presence of the Lord, and from the glory of his power.”**¹⁹

**1. “The end… is for the manifestation of the glory of his mercy, in the eternal salvation of the elect.”** The primary purpose of the judgment is not merely to punish but to reveal God’s glory.²⁰ Romans 9:22-23 declares, “What if God, desiring to show his wrath and to make known his power, has endured with much patience vessels of wrath prepared for destruction, in order to make known the riches of his glory for vessels of mercy, which he has prepared beforehand for glory.”²¹

**2. “And of his justice, in the eternal damnation of the reprobate.”** God’s justice is displayed in the punishment of the wicked. The confession carefully adds that the reprobate are “wicked and disobedient,” showing that their condemnation is not arbitrary but just.²² Romans 6:23 declares, “The wages of sin is death.”²³

**3. “The righteous go into everlasting life, and receive that fulness of joy and glory with everlasting rewards.”** Matthew 25:46 records Jesus’ words: “These will go away into eternal punishment, but the righteous into eternal life.”²⁴

**4. “The wicked… shall be cast aside into everlasting torments, and punished with everlasting destruction.”** The confession affirms the eternal conscious punishment of the wicked. Matthew 25:41 speaks of “the eternal fire prepared for the devil and his angels.”²⁵ 2 Thessalonians 1:9 declares that the wicked “will suffer the punishment of eternal destruction, away from the presence of the Lord and from the glory of his might.”²⁶


#### Paragraph 3 – The Present Impact: Deterrence from Sin, Consolation for the Godly, and Watchfulness

The final paragraph addresses the present practical effects of the doctrine of the last judgment: **“As Christ would have us to be certainly persuaded that there shall be a day of judgment, both to deter all men from sin, and for the greater consolation of the godly in their adversity, so will he have the day unknown to men, that they may shake off all carnal security, and be always watchful, because they know not at what hour the Lord will come, and may ever be prepared to say, Come Lord Jesus; come quickly. Amen.”**²⁷

**1. “To deter all men from sin.”** The certainty of judgment is a deterrent from sin. 2 Corinthians 5:10-11 declares, “For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ… knowing the fear of the Lord, we persuade others.”²⁸

**2. “For the greater consolation of the godly in their adversity.”** The judgment is a comfort to believers who suffer unjustly in this life. 2 Thessalonians 1:5-7 declares that it is “evidence of the righteous judgment of God, that you may be considered worthy of the kingdom of God… since indeed God considers it just to repay with affliction those who afflict you.”²⁹

**3. “So will he have the day unknown to men.”** The confession emphasizes that the day of judgment is unknown. Mark 13:32 declares, “But concerning that day or that hour, no one knows, not even the angels in heaven, nor the Son, but only the Father.”³⁰

**4. “That they may shake off all carnal security, and be always watchful.”** The uncertainty of the hour calls believers to constant readiness. Luke 12:35-40 exhorts, “Stay dressed for action and keep your lamps burning… you also must be ready, for the Son of Man is coming at an hour you do not expect.”³¹

**5. “Come Lord Jesus; come quickly.”** The confession ends with the prayer of the church. Revelation 22:20 records John’s concluding words: “He who testifies to these things says, ‘Surely I am coming soon.’ Amen. Come, Lord Jesus!”³²


### Part 2: Theological Anatomy – The Pillars of the Doctrine of the Last Judgment

Several theological pillars support the confession’s doctrine of the last judgment.

**Pillar 1: The Certainty of the Judgment.** The judgment is not a possibility but an appointment fixed by God. Hebrews 9:27 declares, “It is appointed for man to die once, and after that comes judgment.”³³

**Pillar 2: The Judge Is Christ.** The Father has committed all judgment to the Son (John 5:22). Christ, who suffered for sin, will also judge sinners.³⁴

**Pillar 3: The Universality of the Judgment.** No one escapes. Both apostate angels and all persons who have ever lived will appear before the judgment seat.³⁵

**Pillar 4: Judgment According to Works.** Every thought, word, and deed will be accounted for. Works are not the ground of salvation but the evidence of the state of the soul.³⁶

**Pillar 5: The Purpose Is the Manifestation of God’s Glory.** The judgment displays both God’s mercy (toward the elect) and His justice (toward the reprobate).³⁷

**Pillar 6: Eternal Conscious Punishment.** The confession affirms the traditional doctrine of hell as everlasting torment, not annihilation.³⁸

**Pillar 7: The Present Impact: Deterrence, Consolation, and Watchfulness.** The doctrine of the judgment is not merely future; it shapes present living.³⁹


### Part 3: Pastoral Diagnosis – The Crises of a Denied or Distorted Judgment

Several contemporary crises are directly addressed by Chapter 32.

**Crisis 1: The Denial of a Future Judgment.** Many in modern culture, and even in some theological circles, deny that there will be a final judgment. The confession’s opening words — “God hath appointed a day” — reaffirm this essential truth.⁴⁰

**Crisis 2: Annihilationism.** Some teach that the wicked will cease to exist rather than suffer eternally. The confession affirms “everlasting torments” and “everlasting destruction” — not as cessation of existence, but as separation from the presence of the Lord.⁴¹

**Crisis 3: Universalism.** The belief that all will ultimately be saved undermines the justice of God. The confession declares that the wicked shall be punished “with everlasting destruction.”⁴²

**Crisis 4: The Loss of the Fear of God.** Many live as if there will be no accounting. The confession declares that the certainty of judgment is “to deter all men from sin.”⁴³

**Crisis 5: The Loss of Hope for the Persecuted.** Believers suffering injustice may despair if there is no final judgment. The confession affirms that judgment is for “the greater consolation of the godly in their adversity.”⁴⁴


### Part 4: Practical Return – Living in Light of the Last Judgment

**Step 1: Be Persuaded of the Certainty of the Judgment.** Do not live as if there will be no accounting. Let the certainty of the judgment shape your daily decisions.⁴⁵

**Step 2: Flee from the Wrath to Come.** If you have not trusted Christ, the judgment is a terror. Flee to Christ, the only refuge from the wrath of God.⁴⁶

**Step 3: Find Consolation in the Judgment.** If you suffer unjustly, take heart. The Judge of all the earth will do right. Your vindication is coming.⁴⁷

**Step 4: Shake Off Carnal Security.** Do not presume upon tomorrow. Do not live as if you have plenty of time to repent. The day is certain; the hour is unknown.⁴⁸

**Step 5: Be Watchful.** Keep your lamps burning. Live each day in expectation of the Lord’s return.⁴⁹

**Step 6: Live for the Reward.** The confession speaks of “everlasting rewards.” Your labor in the Lord is not in vain. Let the hope of eternal reward motivate your obedience.⁵⁰

**Step 7: Pray, “Come, Lord Jesus.”** The confession ends with this prayer. Do not fear the day of judgment; long for it. The same day that spells terror for the wicked is the day of your full redemption.⁵¹


### Conclusion: The Day That Rights Every Wrong

Chapter 32 of the 1689 Baptist Confession is not a museum piece of seventeenth-century theology. It is the believer’s anchor in a world of injustice, the sinner’s warning of a coming reckoning, and the church’s prayer for the return of her King. God has appointed a day. Christ will be the Judge. Every person — every thought, word, and deed — will be accounted for. The purpose of the judgment is the manifestation of God’s glory: His mercy in the salvation of the elect, His justice in the damnation of the reprobate. And the present impact of this doctrine is to deter from sin, to console the afflicted, and to call believers to watchfulness, as they wait for that day, praying, “Come, Lord Jesus; come quickly.”⁵²


**A Prayer from Chapter 32** (adapted from the Puritan tradition):

*O God, the righteous Judge of all the earth: We thank Thee that Thou hast appointed a day when every wrong will be righted, every secret revealed, and Thy justice vindicated before the assembled universe. We thank Thee that Christ, who died for our sins, will be the Judge — the same hands that were pierced will hold the throne of judgment. Grant us to be persuaded of the certainty of that day, so that we may flee from sin, find consolation in affliction, and shake off all carnal security. Teach us to be watchful, for we know not the hour. And until that day, let our hearts cry, “Come, Lord Jesus; come quickly.” Amen.*⁵³
